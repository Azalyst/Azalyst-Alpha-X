## 🧠 Flagship Strategy: Bollinger Band Breakout & Pullback

The core strategy implements a **mean-reversion breakout framework** utilizing Bollinger Bands (20, 2) to capture volatility expansions followed by structured pullbacks.

This is NOT a simple "touch and go" system. It requires a specific sequence of events to confirm institutional order flow.

### 🟢 LONG Signal Logic (Upper Band Interaction)

| Phase | Market Condition | Action | Risk Management |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Breakout** | Price closes **above** Upper Bollinger Band | 👁️ Monitor | None |
| **2. Pullback** | Price retraces to **touch** Upper Band | 🎯 Prepare Entry | None |
| **3. Trigger** | Price turns **upward** after touch | 🟢 **BUY** | **SL:** Low of Touch Candle |
| **4. Expansion** | Price moves further above Upper Band | 🏃 Hold Position | Trail/Static SL Active |
| **5. Exit** | Price re-approaches and **touches** Upper Band from above | 🔴 **SELL** (Close Long) | Realize PnL |

**Visual Flow:**
```text

      |                                      (Touch Upper Band)

      |                          (Running) /

      |                       [3] BUY /
      |                      (Turn Up) ^
      |                     /          | SL: Low of Candle
      |           (Break) /            |

      |                /              |
      |               /               |
```

### 🚫 The "No-Man's Land" Rule (Middle Band Protocol)

One of the most critical rules of Azalyst Alpha X:

- **Strict Constraint:** ❌ **NO** trades are initiated or managed while price action is confined between the Upper and Lower Bollinger Bands.
- **Rationale:** The middle zone represents **noise** and mean-reversion chop where statistical edge is negligible.
- **Post-Stop Loss Behavior:** 
  - If a Stop Loss is triggered, the algorithm assumes the price will revert to the Middle Band.
  - The system **DOES NOT INTERFERE** or attempt re-entry.
  - It waits patiently for a **fresh** Breakout-Pullback sequence to establish at the bands.

> **Patience is the edge.** Most losses come from overtrading in the middle band.

## ⚙️ Scanner Configuration & Execution Parameters

The engine operates on a rigid time-cycle to ensure signal freshness and prevent over-trading.

| Parameter | Value | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Candle Timeframe** | `10m` | Primary data granularity for signal generation. |
| **Scan Interval** | `15m` | Frequency of full market scan. |
| **Lookback Window** | `10m` | Signal validity period. Conditions must match within the last 10 minutes at the time of the scan. |
| **Signal Expiry** | `Immediate` | If conditions are not met within the lookback window, the signal is discarded. |

**Execution Cycle:**

```text
[SCAN 1] ----15min----> [SCAN 2] ----15min----> [SCAN 3]
   |                       |                       |
   |--< Lookback 10min -->|                       |
                         |--< Lookback 10min -->|
                                               |--< Lookback 10min -->|
```

1. **WAIT:** Pause for 15 minutes between scans.
2. **SCAN:** Analyze market data for the specific `10m` lookback window.
3. **VALIDATE:** Confirm if `Breakout + Pullback + Trigger` occurred strictly within that window.
4. **EXECUTE:** Fire order if valid; otherwise, discard and wait for next cycle.

> **Why 15m scan with 10m lookback?** 
> This creates a 5-minute buffer to ensure we are catching fresh setups, not stale ones. It filters out noise and ensures high-probability entries.
